PROJECT

Motivation

On my first day at RMIT, I was overwhelmed by how enormous the school is. Even though I had come to visit the university a few times and took a campus tour, I still could not identify what room is where. After almost two months of studying here, I am sometimes still struggling with finding rooms or distinguishing between building 1 and 2. I believe this not only happens for me but a majority of RMIT freshmen have problems in finding ways in our school. A friend of mine even had to skip a test because he could not find where the room was. Due to all the reasons above, I want to create an app that can help students or anyone new to RMIT to find their way around here, and it is called "RMIT guider".

About RMIT Guider

RMIT guider is an app using augmented reality (AR) technology and AI to guide and help people to find their way to the location they want in RMIT. The app will give clear instructions on how to get to a place as well as provide some general information about that room such as name, function, capacity, or interesting facts if has. The user only needs a smartphone with a camera then they can start their adventure in exploring RMIT university.

Features

1 Giving Instructions
There will be a small search box for the user to type in the location where they want to go. It can be a room number (e.g. 2.2.03), the name of the place (e.g. Melbourne Theater, Canteen), a building (e.g. Build 1) or a specific thing (e.g. Vending machine, ATM machine). Besides, if you have no idea about the name or number of that room, you can put in the search box what you want to do then the app can give suggestions to you. For instance, if you want to find a room to study, type in “study room” then the app will recommend you several rooms that are suitable to study. Another example is if you want to find a store which sells curry in RMIT, just type in “curry food”. The app will search if there is any store in RMIT that matches with your requirements then it will suggest you. Since this app stored information about a location, it is not difficult for it to identify a place based on what you provided. Once you finished choosing where you want to go, the app will give instructions right on the screen for you to get there from where you are standing at that moment. For example, if you are standing in front of an intersection, an arrow will pop up on the screen and tell you to turn left, turn right or go straight depending on where that location is.
2Providing Information
When the user holds their camera in front of a room or a location, all the existing essential information about that room will pop up, including name, number, function, capacity, facts, rules, and time. For example, when I am holding my phone camera in front of the library, several boxes will appear on the screen like the picture beside. This can provide basic knowledge about that location for the user so they can know where they are and where to go if they need something. Moreover, the user can know what is allowed and not allowed in that place. Like in some rooms in our school, we are not allowed to bring food and drink inside; if we want to enter a lab, we need to wear sneakers, not flip-flops; and some places are not allowed after a specific time.

The main technology behind this app is AR and GPS. For AR, it will need an AR SDK (software development kit) for image recognition and 3D tracking. There are many platforms that can be used, but I choose ARCore and ARKit so that I can build this app for both Android and IOS devices. It also requires cloud-computing to store information about the places. Besides, this app is a location-based application so it will need an accelerometer or a digital compass to identify the location of the user. Lastly, to create this app, I will need an at least intel core i5 computer with 8GB RAM in order to handle these programs, and I need also a graphic card.

1. What kind of work is done by a Security Specialist?

Since we are living in the 4.0 era, so all most everything in our lives is connected by technology. Our information is stored in smart devices or a database system; the lighting system of our house can be controlled by a smartphone; and sooner or later, technology will help us to do almost everything in life. Therefore, it would be really dangerous if our system is hacked and controlled by outsiders. To prevent that from happening, we need security specialists. Their jobs are building security systems and protecting a system from the attack of hackers or natural incidents. They will have to ensure the system is running probably, anticipate and eliminate any threats to it as well as prevent information to be leaked out. If you are using an automotive car, a security specialist will have to build a security system to protect your car from being hacked and controlled by others.

3. Where does a Security Specialist spend most of his time?

A security specialist works for 8 hours a day and the rest of the time they can go home and relax. At the workplace, he spends most of his time in the office and sometimes in the meeting rooms. If he has more work to do, he can stay at his office to finish them if he prefers.

2. What kinds of people does the a professional interact with?

A Security specialist associates with many other IT professionals such as software engineers, software developers, security analysts, etc. in order to build a proper system. They usually launch meetings to discuss together since there are many parts of a product and each is built by a different IT professional. The security specialist also works with a project manager who gives directives about what tasks need to be done or improved. Last but not least, they have to contact their customers to receive feedback and know if the product satisfies the customer’s demands or not and what needs to be changed.
